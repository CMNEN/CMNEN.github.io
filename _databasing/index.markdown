Databasing is the starting point of any digitization project in the CMNEN collection. The creation of accurate digital records of our specimens helps us to better manage the collection and increases access to specimen data for crucial research on beetle biodiversity. Over the past decade, the CMNEN has used several different databases to enter and manage specimen records, most recently MANTIS and presently EMu. As we continue to refine our workflows for digitization at scale, there will inevitably be a few bumps along the way, but hopefully this site helps keep things on track. 

Use the side menu to get started with databasing.
